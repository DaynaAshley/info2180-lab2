Gold is a chemical element with the symbol Au (from Latin: aurum) and atomic number 79, making it one of the higher atomic number elements that occur naturally. In a pure form, it is a bright, slightly reddish yellow, dense, soft, malleable, and ductile metal. Chemically, gold is a transition metal and a group 11 element. It is one of the least reactive chemical elements and is solid under standard conditions. Introductions are critical to an article, but that doesn't mean you have to brainstorm a new kind of lede for every article. If you build a toolkit of great openings, you can apply the right opening for the right kind of article. The Pain vs. Gain: This is an awesome opening, especially for articles that are about something you've learned through testing or experience. The introduction describes the pain, and builds empathy - gives your audience something to relate to. The Gain is promised, not revealed, so that the audience wants to read the article. For example, if your article is about learning a valuable lesson from almost falling off a roller coaster, the introduction will fall flat if it reveals the lesson you learned.

A blog (a truncation of "weblog") is a discussion or informational website published on the World Wide Web consisting of discrete, often informal diary-style text entries (posts). Posts are typically displayed in reverse chronological order, so that the most recent post appears first, at the top of the web page. Until 2009, blogs were usually the work of a single individual, occasionally of a small group, and often covered a single subject or topic. In the 2010s, "multi-author blogs" (MABs) emerged, featuring the writing of multiple authors and sometimes professionally edited.
